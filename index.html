Requirement Analysis Case Study

An interactive walkthrough of the requirements analysis process for the "SmartMess App for Students".

1. Project Vision & Concept

To empower hostel students with a simple pre-booking system that reduces food wastage, improves meal planning, and ensures a better dining experience for everyone.

**SmartMess** In most Indian hostels, mess food wastage is a daily challenge. Students often skip meals without informing the mess, leading to excess preparation, unnecessary expenses, and wasted food. At the same time, students rarely get a chance to voice their preferences in menu planning. SmartMess addresses this issue through a meal pre-booking system, where students can choose in advance whether they will have a particular meal. This allows the mess staff to prepare food based on actual demand, directly reducing wastage and saving costs. Along with pre-booking, the system includes simple features like viewing the weekly menu, giving daily feedback on meals, and tracking wastage through analytics. By connecting students and mess staff through one transparent platform, SmartMess creates a win-win situation: students get food that matches their choices, staff can plan better with less stress, and the hostel community benefits from reduced wastage and improved quality of meals.

4. Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs) Exploration

We also need to consider *how well* the system should work. These are just as important as the features themselves.

Performance

The weekly mess menu and booking dashboard must load in under 3 seconds on campus Wi-Fi.
The system should support at least 200 concurrent users without slowing down.

Usability

A new student should be able to log in, view the menu, and pre-book a meal within 3 clicks.
The system should have a clean, mobile-friendly interface so students can use it easily from their phones.

Security

All user passwords must be securely hashed and salted before storage.
Role-based access control must be enforced so that students, staff, and admins only access their respective features.

Reliability

The system should be available 99.5% of the time during meal booking hours.
In case of downtime, the system should recover and restore booking data without loss.

5. Preparing for Sprint 1: Detailing a User Story

Now, we take a high-priority "Must Have" story and add **Acceptance Criteria** to make it ready for development. This defines the "Definition of Done".

User Story:

As a Student,I want to view the weekly mess menu, so that I can decide which meals to book .

Acceptance Criteria:

Scenario 1: Viewing the menu

Given I am logged into the system, When I open the "Weekly Menu" page, Then I should see a section titled "Weekly Menu".

Scenario 2: Menu displays correct information

Given I am viewing the "Weekly Mess Menu" section, When I look at a meal entry, Then I should see the meal name,day, date, and time.

Scenario 3: Menu is in chronological order

Given I am viewing the "Weekly mess menu" section, When I scroll through at the list, Then the meals should be sorted by day qnd meal time in proper order.

Scenario 4: No Mneu Available

Given the weekly menu hasnt been added by the staff, And I open the weekly mess menu page, Then the page loads, Then I should see a message that says "Mneu not available."

6. Summary of Final Requirements

This section consolidates all the analysis into a clear, final list of requirements for the first version of the SmartMess System.

Functional Requirements (Product Backlog)

  • User Management: Students must be able to register and log in to access meal booking. Staff and Admin must log in with their credentials to manage menu, bookings, and wastage.
  • Menu & Meal Booking: Students must be able to view the weekly mess menu, pre-book meals, and modify or cancel bookings before the cutoff time.
  • Booking Summary: Mess staff must be able to view the total number of pre-booked meals for each session (breakfast, lunch, dinner).
  • Feedback System: Students must be able to rate meals and give comments, and staff must be able to view summarized feedback.
  • Wastage Tracking: Staff must be able to enter daily leftover quantities to monitor wastage levels.
  • Analytics & Reports: Admin must be able to view charts comparing bookings vs wastage and export reports.
  • Admin Functions: Admin must be able to update the weekly menu and manage user roles (student, staff, admin).
  • Enhancements: Students should be able to suggest dishes, vote on menu suggestions, and optionally earn reward points for consistent pre-booking.

  • Usability: A student should be able to log in, view the menu, and pre-book a meal in 3 clicks or less; interface should be mobile-friendly.
  • Security: All passwords must be securely hashed and salted; role-based access must be enforced.
  • Reliability: The system must be available at least 99.5% of the time during booking hours, and recover without losing booking data in case of downtime..
